When Kait met Sally

In June 2016, Sally made the move from Charleston, SC to Durham, joining the rest of the Hall family (her parents and four siblings), who were all settled in the Bull City. Over Labor Day weekend that same year, Kait moved to Durham from Wilmington, NC, also to be closer to her parents and sister. At the time, neither of them suspected in the slightest that by the end of September, they would meet and immediately fall for each other.

After talking online and via text for a few weeks, Kait suggested meeting at all-women stand-up comedy show at the Pinhook. Even though it meant getting a baby-sitter on a school night, Sally had a feeling this date might be worth it. Kait and Sally were sitting on a couch, chatting over a beer before show started when a staff member asked if he could move their seat closer to the stage. Their couch ended up on the front row where they shared it with another couple. For the next few hours, they laughed, bantered with the comics, and talked between sets. In such close quarters, it was inevitable to make some physical contact, but when Sally reached over and took Kait’s hand, they both knew the night had not only been worth it, but was the beginning of something much more than a date.
